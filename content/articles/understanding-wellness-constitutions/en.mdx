In this project, **constitution** refers to body constitution, wellness constitution, or 体质 in a traditional Chinese wellness sense. It does **not** mean a legal or political constitution. That distinction matters, because the entire recommendation experience on this site is built around helping users understand everyday body tendencies in a gentle, non-medical way.

Constitution is useful because it gives people a framework for noticing patterns. Instead of asking only, “What feels uncomfortable today?” it also asks, “What kind of body pattern seems to show up again and again?” That shift helps explain why two people with similar complaints may respond better to different kinds of support.

## 1. Introduction

In traditional Chinese wellness thinking, a constitution describes the overall tendencies of a person’s body rather than a single symptom. It includes things like how someone tends to handle temperature, rest, digestion, stress, dryness, heaviness, and recovery.

A constitution is not a fixed label for life. It is better understood as an orientation. Some tendencies may feel more prominent during one season, one period of stress, or one stage of life than another. That is one reason constitution awareness can be so helpful: it encourages observation rather than rigid identity.

## 2. Why constitution matters

Constitution is foundational in traditional wellness logic because it shifts attention from isolated discomforts to broader patterns.

Two people may both say they feel tired, for example, but the underlying tendencies may look different:

- one person may feel depleted, cold, and slow to recover
- another may feel tired but restless, dry, and overheated
- another may feel fatigued mainly after meals and accompanied by heaviness

If those patterns differ, the most suitable tea style, ingredient profile, and daily routine may differ too. This is why constitution-based thinking remains such an important part of traditional wellness systems. It tries to understand the context around a feeling, not just the feeling itself.

## 3. Why the AI helper asks multiple questions

The AI helper does not jump directly to a recommendation because a single answer rarely tells the whole story. A more thoughtful recommendation needs more than one signal.

The intake questions are designed to gather practical clues such as:

- energy level across the day
- temperature preference and sensitivity to cold or heat
- digestion tendencies
- sleep patterns
- dryness or dampness tendencies
- stress and emotional patterns
- recurring discomforts
- lifestyle habits and daily rhythm

This is meant to feel like a guided wellness intake, not a medical interrogation. The purpose is to build a broader picture of how someone tends to feel, so the first recommendation can be more personalized and less random.

## 4. How those answers support a preliminary constitution assessment

The AI helper uses those intake responses to estimate likely body tendencies. In this project, that may include orientations such as:

- **Qi deficiency**: often associated with low energy, easy fatigue, or feeling underpowered
- **Yang deficiency**: may suggest a tendency toward coldness, low warmth, and sluggishness
- **Yin deficiency**: may suggest dryness, internal heat, or a “worn but wired” feeling
- **Phlegm-dampness**: may suggest heaviness, sluggishness, or a sense of fullness
- **Damp-heat**: may suggest a mix of heaviness and heat-like irritability
- **Qi stagnation**: may suggest tension, emotional constraint, or a stuck feeling
- **Blood stasis**: may suggest a more fixed, stagnant, or obstructed pattern
- **Qi and blood deficiency**: may suggest a broader pattern of depletion and insufficient nourishment

These are **preliminary wellness interpretations**, not diagnoses. They offer orientation. They can help the system decide which tea families, ingredients, and daily ritual suggestions are more likely to feel appropriate as a starting point.

## 5. Benefits of knowing your constitution

Understanding your constitution can be useful in everyday life, even if you are only using it as a gentle wellness lens.

It may help you:

- choose teas or herbal products more thoughtfully
- understand why certain habits suit you better than others
- become more aware of seasonal changes in your body
- recognize repeated patterns in fatigue, digestion, mood, dryness, or heaviness
- build a more personalized daily wellness routine
- use AI helper recommendations with more context and confidence

In other words, constitution awareness makes personalization more meaningful. It turns tea selection into something more considered than simply choosing whatever sounds nice in the moment.

## 6. How users should use this information

The healthiest way to use constitution knowledge is as a practical framework for observation.

It can help to ask:

- Which patterns seem to repeat for me?
- What kinds of teas do I naturally feel better with?
- Do my preferences change with season, stress, or sleep?
- Which routines help me feel more balanced over time?

Constitution awareness works best when it stays flexible. It should invite curiosity, not fear. It should support more personalized wellness choices, not become a rigid box.
